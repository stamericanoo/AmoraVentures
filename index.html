Sulfuric Acid Plant – 1000 TPD

Comprehensive Industrial Process & Economic Feasibility Study

Executive Summary

This project presents the full industrial-scale design of a Sulfuric Acid plant with a production capacity of 1000 tons per day using the Contact Process. The study integrates chemical reaction engineering, mass and energy balance, equipment design considerations, capital investment estimation, operational cost analysis, and long-term financial feasibility evaluation.

The plant operates 330 days per year and achieves approximately 98% overall conversion efficiency using a multi-bed V₂O₅ catalytic system. Heat recovery integration ensures optimized energy efficiency while minimizing operational cost.

1. Sulfur Burning Furnace

Main Reaction: S + O₂ → SO₂

Operating Temperature: 900–1100°C Pressure: Slightly above atmospheric Conversion Efficiency: >99%

This highly exothermic reaction releases approximately -296 kJ/mol. Heat is recovered through waste heat boilers generating steam.

2. Gas Cleaning & Drying

The SO₂ gas stream is cooled to 40–80°C. Dust and impurities are removed using cyclones and electrostatic precipitators. Moisture removal is critical to prevent acid mist formation.

3. Catalytic Converter

2SO₂ + O₂ ⇌ 2SO₃

Temperature: 420–450°C Pressure: 1–2 atm Catalyst: V₂O₅ Efficiency: 96–98%

The reaction is reversible and exothermic. Temperature control ensures equilibrium shift toward SO₃ formation.

4. Absorption Tower

SO₃ is absorbed into concentrated sulfuric acid to form oleum: SO₃ + H₂SO₄ → H₂S₂O₇

Operating Temperature: 60–80°C Pressure: Atmospheric

5. Dilution & Final Product Formation

H₂S₂O₇ + H₂O → 2H₂SO₄ Highly exothermic hydration reaction. Cooling systems prevent overheating.

Sensitivity Analysis

Sulfur price fluctuation and conversion efficiency significantly affect IRR. +10% sulfur cost reduces IRR to ~15%. Improved efficiency increases profitability.

Environmental & Safety Analysis

Emission control systems ensure SO₂ emissions below 200 mg/Nm³. Double absorption minimizes environmental impact. Safety systems include pressure relief valves, temperature monitoring, and emergency scrubbers.
